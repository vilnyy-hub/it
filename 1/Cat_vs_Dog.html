Nutrition Guide & Feeding Norms

1. Daily portion of dry food (by weight)

Dog weightPortion (g)
Small (up to 10 kg)80–150 g
Medium (10–25 kg)160–300 g
Large (25–45 kg)310–480 g
Giant (45+ kg)500–650 g

*For very active dogs, the portion can be increased by 20–30%.

2. Proportions of natural nutrition

  • 60–70% — Proteins: meat (beef, turkey, chicken) and offal (heart, stomach).
  • 15–25% — Vegetables: carrots, zucchini, beets.
  • 10–15% — Porridge: buckwheat, rice, oatmeal.

3. Daily meat allowance

For medium breeds (10-25 kg) — 100-200g; for large breeds — 200-400g of meat per day.

4. Feeding frequency

Puppies: 3-4 times. Adults: 2 times (morning/evening). Small dogs: often, but in small portions.

Safety Note: If your dog is prone to picking up food from the ground or has allergies, be sure to teach it to wear a muzzle and the "Leave it!" command.

Training & Safety

Train the "Leave it" command early. Use positive reinforcement to make muzzle training a happy experience!

Walking Gear & Comfort

  • Every dog requires a different collar or harness size tailored to their body measurements for safety and comfort.
  • If your dog tends to pull on the leash, using a harness is much better than a traditional collar to prevent throat strain and ensure optimal control.
